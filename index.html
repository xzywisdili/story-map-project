Bus shelters are little beacons of everyday life—queues, quick bites, coffee, umbrellas on rainy days. They hint at steady foot traffic, the kind that keeps small vendors afloat.

Takeaway: Compare these dots with the “no-go” layers. Are we pushing vendors away from where riders actually are?

Recreation districts stitch together fields, trails, and playgrounds—weekend tournaments, summer camps, movie nights in the park. They’re community magnets.

Takeaway: When vending rules meet park life, the question becomes: do we enable low-cost treats and services near these community hubs—or fence them out?
